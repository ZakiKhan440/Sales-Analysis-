Background

Context & Objective

Currently, approximately 30 towns in the Peshawar region are served through a Master Distributor (MD) setup. The MD receives stock from Islamabad, supplies multiple sub-distributors, and handles order consolidation, inventory visibility, and market responsiveness — charging a 3.75% margin plus actual freight costs.

This analysis evaluates whether the company should directly serve Pabbi's's top 4 towns — Mardan, Kohat, Nowshera, and Bannu — by comparing the MD margin cost against direct distribution (DD) logistics overhead and weighing the exit risks involved.

Strategic Options

Scenario Analysis: MD vs. Direct Distribution

Two paths were evaluated. The evaluation criteria weighs MD margin cost versus DD logistics and overhead, while also accounting for MD exit risks, service continuity for smaller adjacent towns, and retailer credit exposure.

Conclusion

Key Findings & Recommendation

The top 4 towns represent significant financial scale (PKR 64M of 132M) and are logistically clustered, making them viable DD candidates. However, the transition requires careful sequencing to manage MD exit risk and SD readiness.

Analyst Recommendation
A phased transition to direct distribution for Mardan and Nowshera is recommended as the first stage, given their volume and logistical proximity. Kohat and Bannu should follow once sub-distributor stock capacity and warehousing requirements are confirmed. The MD should be retained for the remaining 26 towns to avoid service disruption.
